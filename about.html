Who we are

Port Moresby General Hospital (PMGH) is Papua New Guinea’s largest public hospital and the main national referral centre. The hospital provides specialist medical, surgical, maternity, paediatric and diagnostic services for patients from Port Moresby, the National Capital District and provinces across the country.

PMGH is closely linked with the University of Papua New Guinea – School of Medicine & Health Sciences and serves as a major teaching and training site for medical students, nursing students and postgraduate trainees.

Our mission

To deliver safe, accessible and high-quality healthcare to the people of Papua New Guinea while supporting clinical training, research and continuous improvement in the health system.

Our vision

To be a modern, efficient and patient-centred national referral hospital that provides excellence in clinical care, education and research.

Our values

  • Compassion: Treating all patients and families with dignity and respect.
  • Integrity: Acting honestly, ethically and professionally in all we do.
  • Teamwork: Working together across disciplines and with partners.
  • Excellence: Striving to continuously improve the quality of care.
  • Accountability: Using public resources responsibly and transparently.

Our role in the health system

As the national referral hospital, PMGH provides specialist services that are not widely available elsewhere in the country. The hospital supports provincial and district hospitals through clinical advice, referral pathways and training opportunities.

PMGH also contributes to national programs in areas such as maternal and child health, infectious diseases, surgery, trauma, non-communicable diseases and public health initiatives.
