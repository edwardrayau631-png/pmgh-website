Overview

Port Moresby General Hospital (PMGH) is the largest public hospital in Papua New Guinea. Located in Korobosea, National Capital District, it serves as the primary referral hospital for the country and provides essential healthcare to the population of Port Moresby and surrounding regions.

PMGH operates as a key teaching hospital in partnership with the University of Papua New Guinea – School of Medicine and Health Sciences (SMHS). The hospital plays a vital role in training medical doctors, nurses, and health professionals through clinical placements, residency programs, and specialist training.

Our Mission

To deliver safe, accessible, and high-quality healthcare to all people of Papua New Guinea, while advancing medical training, research, and clinical excellence.

Our Vision

To be a modern, efficient, and patient-focused national referral hospital, committed to raising the standard of healthcare, improving patient outcomes, and strengthening the country’s health workforce.

Core Values

  • Compassion: Treating patients with dignity, respect, and care.
  • Integrity: Upholding professionalism and ethical practice.
  • Teamwork: Working together across departments and partners.
  • Excellence: Continuously improving the quality of healthcare services.
  • Accountability: Responsible and transparent use of public resources.

Role in the Health System

As PNG’s main referral hospital, PMGH provides specialist care including emergency medicine, surgery, internal medicine, obstetrics and gynaecology, paediatrics, intensive care, diagnostic imaging, pathology, dental services, mental health care, and surgical subspecialties.

PMGH also supports major national health programs such as maternal and child health, infectious disease control, cancer services, blood donation, and community health education.

Teaching & Training Hospital

PMGH is a central partner for medical education in PNG. Student doctors, nurses, community health workers, and postgraduate trainees receive hands-on experience in all clinical disciplines. The hospital regularly hosts specialist attachments, external programs, and overseas collaborations aimed at improving the country’s clinical capacity.
